This Privacy Policy explains how the Emotion Thermometer website (“we”, “our”, or “the site”) collects, uses, and stores information when you use our service. We take your privacy seriously and collect only what is necessary to make the tool work.

1. What we collect

We collect and store the following data:

  • Google account information — when you sign in with Google we receive your name, email address, and profile picture URL. This is used only to identify your account so your history can be saved and retrieved.
  • Emotion thermometer entries — the feelings, physical signals, thoughts, behaviors, and coping strategies you write into the thermometer form. These are stored in our database linked to your account and are visible only to you.
  • Basic usage data — the date and time an entry was saved, and which page template was used. We do not collect IP addresses, device identifiers, or behavioral analytics.

2. How we use your data

Your data is used exclusively to provide the core functionality of the site:

  • Displaying your saved thermometer entries when you return to the site.
  • Syncing your history across different devices when you are signed in.
  • Generating PDF exports of your entries on request.

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, or any purpose beyond what is described above.

3. Data storage and security

Your entries are stored in a cloud database. We use encrypted connections (HTTPS) for all data in transit. Access to your entries is restricted to your authenticated account — no other user can view your data. Our infrastructure is hosted on Vercel and Turso, both of which operate industry-standard security practices.

When you are not signed in, entries are stored only in your browser’s local storage and never sent to our servers.

4. Data sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with any third parties. The only external service that receives your data is Google, solely for the purpose of authenticating your identity during sign-in. We do not share your thermometer entries with any third party under any circumstances.

5. Data retention and deletion

Your thermometer entries are kept for as long as your account is active. You can delete individual entries at any time using the “×” button next to each saved entry — this removes the entry from our database immediately. You can also clear your entire history using the “Clear history” button on the main page.

If you would like your account and all associated data permanently deleted, please contact us at the email address below and we will remove everything within 30 days.

6. Cookies and local storage

We use a secure, HTTP-only session cookie to keep you signed in after you authenticate with Google. This cookie contains a signed token and does not store any personal information directly. It expires when you sign out or after a reasonable period of inactivity.

We also use your browser’s local storage to remember your current thermometer draft and your saved name between visits. This data stays on your device and is not sent to our servers unless you are signed in.

7. Children’s privacy

This tool is intended to be used by or with the guidance of a parent, guardian, or mental health professional. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under the age of 13 without parental consent. If you believe a child has submitted personal information through this site, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the “Last updated” date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

9. Contact

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or how your data is handled, please reach out via the project repository or open an issue there.
